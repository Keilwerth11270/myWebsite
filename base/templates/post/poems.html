My Poetry

Adam

Breath The beginning of man For when Adam was but a vessel of dust And The Eternal gave him the breath of life That is when he was born And the breath of life was a gift And the breath of life gave Adam his soul And the breath of life gave Adam consciousness And the descendants of Adam When they breathe Do they not remember The miracle that is life? So breathe as your father Adam did And do not deprive the soul of its desire To live and breathe As the breath is the genesis of the soul And breath is the source of life Breath is the start of it all

Jody

Say: When the signs arrive and I am asked to change, will I be able? ' Say: And as the notes of time mark my path, will I keep up? ' Say: Those who learn to breathe, verily they will never lose track ' Say: So breathe, and take in everything ' Say: I can bear it all ' Say: I can do it. ' Now let it all go. The piece is complete. Lost in the blue Sparks shrouded in a gray mist Coming closer and closer and closer You cry out "Where is our Lord?" Thunder rolls and lightning strikes The tithe must be given A child overboard "Is this what You wanted? "Dear Lord, help us if You are there!" The sea, satisfied with this sacrifice Pushes you along Parents crying Yet relieved The storm has passed "Praise God, for relieving us of this suffering!" The Holy Land in sight Romans at the entrance of the Temple Demanding payment While throwing their food scraps on the consecrated earth Have we not lost enough?

A New Caesar

Lost in the blue Sparks shrouded in a gray mist Coming closer and closer and closer You cry out "Lord, save us from this trial!" Thunder rolls and lightning strikes The tithe must be given A child overboard "Oh Lord, hear the howling of these poor parents! "Remember when you took Jonah, "And protected him inside the stomach of a fish. "Remember when you accepted his prayers "And commanded the fish to spit him out, "Holy Rabb, we beg of you to spit us out of this storm!" The sea, satisfied with this sacrifice Pushes you along Parents crying Yet relieved The storm has passed "Praise God, for relieving us of this suffering!" Distant A black flag Arriving safely "Rejoice! The Romans are gone!" Jerusalem As beautiful as the ancients described it The miraculous pilgrimage Brought us to the Holy Land And the prayers of the paupers Who lost their child to the sea Heard We are home.
